2. The Exact Insertion Identity

2.1 Statement and Proof

Theorem 1. For any composite n вүҘ 4 with PвҒ»(n) = p and m = n/p, G_spf(n) = j(m) + K_p(m), where j(m) = max(G(m), 0) and K_p(m) = ПҒ_E(pmвҲ’1) вҲ’ ПҒ_E(mвҲ’1) вҲ’ ПҒ_E(p) вҲ’ 2.

Proof. By definition, G_spf(n) = ПҒ_E(nвҲ’1) вҲ’ ПҒ_E(p) вҲ’ ПҒ_E(m) вҲ’ 1. The DP recurrence gives ПҒ_E(m) = ПҒ_E(mвҲ’1) + 1 вҲ’ j(m). Substituting:

Remark. When m is prime, G(m) вүӨ 0, so j(m) = 0 and ПҒ_E(m) = ПҒ_E(mвҲ’1) + 1, consistent. Verified: N = 10вҒ·, k = 3 through 18, identity holds exactly for all composites.

2.2 The Insertion Recursion

The map (m, p) вҶҰ n = pm with О©(m) = k, p prime, p вүӨ PвҒ»(m), pm вүӨ x gives a bijection onto {n вүӨ x : О©(n) = k+1, n composite}. Define the insertion measure I_k(x) by this bijection. Then:

This is exact. The first term transmits the previous-layer jump; the second is the local bridge cost.

2.3 Interpretation: the Relay Formalized

Paper 15 В§6.4 identified the relay mechanism qualitatively: P(jump) drives at low k, E[j/ln | jump] drives at high k. The insertion identity reveals the precise algebraic mechanism: the (k+1)-th layer's mean gain inherits the k-th layer's jump sizes (via j(m)), augmented or diminished by the bridge cost K_p(m).

Proof. A(pm) = ПҒ_E(pmвҲ’1) вҲ’ ПҒ_E(pm). B(pm) = ПҒ_E(pm) вҲ’ ПҒ_E(p) вҲ’ ПҒ_E(m) вҲ’ 2. A(m) = ПҒ_E(mвҲ’1) вҲ’ ПҒ_E(m). Therefore:

9. Toward Convergence of SPF-Positivity Rates
