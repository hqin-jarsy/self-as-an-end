ZFCρ Paper LXII: Two-River Synchronization and the Precise Restatement of Spectral Ratio Boundedness — From P+R Decomposition to Conjecture 59.1
ZFCρ 第LXII篇：双河同步与谱比有界性的精确重述——从 P+R 分解到 Conjecture 59.1

Han Qin (秦汉)  ·  Independent Researcher  ·  2026
DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19656366  ·  Full PDF on Zenodo  ·  CC BY 4.0
Abstract

Paper LXII presents the "Two-River Synchronization" model and restates Conjecture 59.1 (spectral ratio boundedness) in a precise, testable form. The P+R decomposition (principal + remainder) is used to isolate the structural sources of spectral ratio growth. The paper demonstrates that the two spectral rivers — the deterministic dlp^k weights and the arithmetic correlations of h(p)/p — exhibit synchronized decay, providing the structural foundation for the O(1) bound asserted by Conjecture 59.1. A precise restatement of 59.1 is given in terms of the P+R decomposition, clarifying what the conjecture actually requires and what would constitute a proof.

Keywords: ZFCρ, spectral ratio boundedness, P+R decomposition, Two-River Synchronization, Conjecture 59.1, H prime
