Abstract

This paper establishes a conditional theorem on the weak mixing angle within the Self-as-an-End (SAE) framework. The sole fully gauge-trivial component is the right-handed neutrino ($\nu_R$, $y_{\rm SAE} = 0$), which serves as the group-theoretic identity of the four-bit encoding. $$\sin^2\theta_W\big|_{\text{DD eigen}} = \frac{2^4 - 1}{C(12,2) - 1} = \frac{15}{65} = \frac{3}{13} \approx 0.2308$$

The experimental value $\hat{s}_Z^2(M_Z) = 0.23122 \pm 0.00006$ (PDG 2025) differs from the DD eigenvalue by $+4.51 \times 10^{-4}$ (relative deviation $-0.195\%$, approximately $7.5\sigma$), consistent in magnitude with one-loop electroweak radiative corrections. This constitutes a conditional theorem whose conditions are the Paper II one-generation hypercharge table, the Prequel's structural capacity 65, and the EW Matching Axiom above. The axiom belongs to the same class of structure-to-EFT bridge axioms as the Prequel's $\kappa = 65/4$ postulate.

§1. Introduction

The SAE framework measures 1DD's presence using 1DD's own natural measure. By the "labeling without constructing" property of 1DD, this measure is binary counting: $\sum 1 = 15$.

§6.4 The DD eigenvalue

Definition (DD eigenvalue). The DD eigenvalue of an EFT parameter is the value of that parameter at the DD structural level — considering only the combinatorial structure and the static P1/P2 allocation, without remainder dynamic fluctuations.

Properties of the DD eigenvalue:

  1. Scheme-independent. The DD eigenvalue is a combinatorial count ratio of DD structure, involving no regularization, renormalization, or cutoff. Different renormalization schemes only affect how remainder dynamic fluctuations are computed, not the DD structure itself.
  2. Unique. For a given DD structure and DD layer, the DD eigenvalue is uniquely determined by the combinatorial structure.
  3. Not equivalent to the SM bare value. The SM bare value depends on the regularization scheme and cutoff and is not a physical quantity. The DD eigenvalue is scheme-independent and intrinsic to the DD structure.

The core claim of this paper: the DD eigenvalue of the weak mixing angle is

$$\sin^2\theta_W\big|_{\text{DD eigen}} = \frac{15}{65} = \frac{3}{13}$$

The relation between the experimentally measurable $\hat{s}_Z^2(M_Z)$ and the DD eigenvalue is:

$$\hat{s}_Z^2(M_Z) = \frac{3}{13} + \delta s^2_{\text{fluct}}(M_Z)$$

where $\delta s^2_{\text{fluct}}(M_Z)$ is the contribution of remainder dynamic fluctuations at the energy scale $M_Z$. In SM language, this corresponds to electroweak radiative corrections (W/Z self-energies, fermion loops, Higgs loops, etc.).

On the choice of term "eigenvalue." In linear algebra, an eigenvalue is the pure scaling factor of a linear transformation on its eigenvector, after all rotations and mixings have been stripped away. The DD eigenvalue is the pure structural ratio of DD structure acting on its own native remainder, after all remainder dynamic fluctuations have been stripped away. The logical isomorphism is exact: eigenvalue = the one thing that the structure does to its own native object once all non-intrinsic effects are removed.
