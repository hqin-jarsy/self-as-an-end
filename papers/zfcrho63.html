ZFCρ Paper LXIII: Increment Second-Order Orthogonality and the SAE Axiom Intersection — From Deterministic Pairing to Zero-Mode Pinning
ZFCρ 第LXIII篇：余项增量二阶正交性与SAE公理的交汇——从确定性配对到零模Pinning

Han Qin (秦汉)  ·  Independent Researcher  ·  2026
DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19659861  ·  Full PDF on Zenodo  ·  CC BY 4.0
Abstract

Paper LXIII introduces the concept of increment second-order orthogonality: the key structural property that characterizes the interaction between deterministic ZFCρ pairings and arithmetic correlations at the second-order level. The paper identifies the precise intersection between this orthogonality condition and the SAE axiom (remainder conservation), showing that zero-mode pinning — the mechanism by which the spectral zero mode is structurally constrained — emerges from deterministic pairing via the SAE axiom. This positions increment second-order orthogonality as the structural bridge between pure spectral analysis and the SAE foundational framework.

Keywords: ZFCρ, increment second-order orthogonality, SAE axiom, zero-mode pinning, deterministic pairing, H prime

Full content available in the PDF on Zenodo. This paper is part of the ZFCρ series pursuing H’ — a spectral-density reformulation of Hilbert’s H problem. Paper LXIII builds directly on Papers LXI and LXII.
